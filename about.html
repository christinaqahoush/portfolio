About Hi, I'm Christina

Art rooted in memory, nature, and the landscapes of Palestine

I create visual artworks that explore the relationship between humans and nature, focusing on vanishing Palestinian flora and fauna. Through Cyanotype and collage techniques, I preserve fragments of memory, land, and identity, expressing how environment, history, and personal experience intertwine.

How I Got Here

My path as an artist began in Ramallah, where I studied at Saint Joseph School and later continued my academic journey at Birzeit University, specializing in Fine Arts. These early experiences shaped my connection to the natural world and to the cultural landscapes around me. Over time, my work evolved toward exploring the relationship between humans and nature, focusing on vanishing Palestinian flora and fauna through Cyanotype and collage techniques. Each piece reflects a process of preserving memory, identity, and the fragile environment that surrounds us.

My Values & Beliefs

Preservation of Nature

I believe in the importance of documenting and preserving the fragile ecosystems around us. Much of my work focuses on vanishing Palestinian flora and fauna, capturing what is at risk of being forgotten.

Memory as Inspiration

My artistic process is deeply tied to memory—personal, cultural, and collective. I use Cyanotype and collage to explore how memories shape our understanding of the natural world and our place within it.

How I Create

Each artwork begins with collecting natural materials—plants, textures, and found objects—that carry a story. Through Cyanotype printing and layered collage, I transform these fragments into visual narratives that are both true to their origin and emotionally resonant.

I value honesty in process, ensuring that each piece reflects careful observation and faithful representation. Accuracy in capturing details allows the viewer to connect deeply with the subject while experiencing the nuances of memory, time, and nature.
